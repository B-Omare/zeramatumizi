# ZeraMatumizi Monthly Situation Report

**Monthly County Situation Report for Kisumu County, June 2026**

1. **Executive Summary**: Kisumu County reports a disorder progression rate of 43.5%, exceeding the national average of 39.2%. The prevalence of substance use, including alcohol (35.1%) and cannabis (9.2%), poses a significant concern. This report highlights key risk indicators, digital surveillance findings, and evidence-based recommendations to inform resource allocation decisions.

2. **Key Risk Indicators**:
* Disorder progression rate: 43.5%
* Alcohol use rate: 35.1%
* Cannabis use rate: 9.2%
* Khat use rate: 12.2%
* Unemployment rate: 47.8%

3. **Digital Surveillance Findings**: Analysis of 35 online posts reveals a notable presence of substance-related discussions, including illicit brew (6 posts), cannabis (6 posts), and khat (4 posts). Additionally, posts related to treatment (4 posts), distress (4 posts), and policy (4 posts) suggest a need for targeted interventions and support services.

4. **Evidence-Based Recommendations**: 
* Implement targeted NACADA campaigns to reduce disorder registrations, leveraging the proven 27% reduction in treated counties.
* Advocate for policies to restrict legal alcohol access to individuals below the age of 18, considering the 56% increased disorder risk associated with early access.
* Develop and disseminate public health messages highlighting the causal link between frequent drinking and increased disorder severity, utilizing the NACADA helpline (1192) as a resource for support and guidance.

5. **Resources**: Individuals seeking support or information can contact the NACADA helpline at 1192. This resource can be leveraged to provide guidance, referrals, and support to those affected by substance use disorders in Kisumu County.
